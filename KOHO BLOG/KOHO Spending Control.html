How KOHO Helps You Track and Control Your Spending Habits

KOHO offers a helping hand with that. See Your Spending Instantly with Real-Time Alerts

One of the most frustrating parts of managing money? That awkward lag where you spend and have to wait for your bank to catch up. Sometimes, it takes days to see a charge show up, which just adds to the confusion and can leave you overspending without realizing it—or getting hit with a dreaded overdraft fee.

With KOHO, the moment you use your prepaid Mastercard, you get a notification on your phone, letting you know exactly how much you spent and where. No more waiting, no more “Did I really buy that?” moments.

Here’s why these instant alerts make such a difference:

  • Stay in the Loop: You always know your up-to-date balance, so there’s less temptation to overspend.
  • A Nudge When You Need It: Seeing each purchase pop up is a gentle reminder to stick to your plan—like a digital tap on the shoulder.
  • Built-in Security: If someone else uses your card, you’ll know right away and can take action quickly to protect your money.

Imagine paying for lunch and before your food even hits the table, you get a message: “You just spent $15.50 at The Corner Cafe.” It’s simple, it’s clear, and it keeps your spending transparent.

Get the Full Picture with Automatic Expense Categories

Scrolling through a sea of transactions makes it tough to spot spending patterns—or figure out where you can cut back. KOHO simplifies this by automatically grouping your expenses. Whether you’re at the grocery store, paying for a ride, or shopping online, every purchase gets filed under a clear category.

Why does this matter? Here’s what automatic sorting does for you:

  • Understand Instantly: No need for complicated spreadsheets. You get a breakdown by category, right in your app.
  • Spot Patterns: Maybe you’re spending more on takeout than you thought, or those streaming subscriptions are adding up. KOHO’s categories help you notice trends you might otherwise miss.
  • Smarter Choices: Once you know where your money’s going, you can set a budget that fits your real life. Say your “Shopping” category is looking a little high—you can challenge yourself to bring it down next month and track it as you go.

Think of it as an always-updating financial snapshot. With each purchase, you learn a bit more about your habits, helping you adjust and move your money toward what matters to you most.

Create Budgets That Actually Work for You

Making a budget shouldn’t feel like setting up a list of rules you’re doomed to break. Instead, think of it as a plan that gives you room to enjoy life while steering your money where it counts.

KOHO’s budgeting tools are easy to use and run right in the app. You can see, at a glance, what you’ve spent so far in the month, and how it stacks up against your income or your limits for different categories. Planning to spend no more than $300 on eating out? As you grab dinner or order takeout, you can check your app to see how much of your food budget is left. It’s flexible and lets you make choices in real time.

With this approach, budgeting becomes less of a chore and more of a daily check-in. It’s less about guilt and more about empowerment—making sure your money is moving in the direction you want.
