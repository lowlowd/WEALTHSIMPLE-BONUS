Getting Started: Banking Without the Barriers

One of the first hurdles for many newcomers is opening a traditional bank account. Big banks often require a credit history, proof of address, and a lot of paperwork, which can be difficult to provide when you’ve just arrived.

KOHO makes getting started incredibly simple. You can sign up for an account in minutes right from your phone, often without needing a credit history. This easy and accessible process means you can have a working Canadian spending account almost immediately, allowing you to focus on other important parts of settling in.

Say Goodbye to Confusing Bank Fees

When you're managing your finances in a new country, the last thing you need are surprise charges eating into your budget. Many traditional bank accounts come with monthly fees, charges for sending money, and steep penalties if your account balance gets too low.

KOHO was built on a foundation of transparency and fairness, which is perfect for anyone trying to make every dollar count.

  • No Monthly Fees: The standard KOHO account is completely free. You don’t have to pay a monthly fee just to have an account.
  • Free e-Transfers: Sending money to a landlord, friend, or family member in Canada is easy with Interac e-Transfer. With KOHO, these transfers are free and unlimited.
  • No Overdraft Worries: KOHO is a prepaid system, which means you can only spend the money you’ve loaded onto your account. This is a huge relief because it’s impossible to accidentally overdraw your account and get hit with a non-sufficient funds (NSF) fee, which can cost over $45 at a traditional bank.

With KOHO, you have a straightforward, fee-free platform. This lets you manage your money with confidence, knowing there won't be any hidden costs.

Master Your Budget in a New Country

Managing a budget is challenging for everyone, but it’s especially tricky when you're getting used to a new currency and cost of living. The KOHO app is designed to be your personal budgeting assistant, giving you the clarity you need to stay on track.

  • Real-Time Spending Insights: The moment you use your KOHO card, you get an instant notification on your phone. The app automatically organizes your spending into categories like "Groceries," "Rent," or "Shopping." This gives you a clear, visual picture of where your money is going, helping you adjust your budget as you learn the costs of living in Canada.
  • Save Effortlessly with RoundUps: This feature is perfect for building savings without even trying. Every time you make a purchase, KOHO can round up the amount to the nearest dollar (or more) and put the spare change into your savings. A $3.75 coffee automatically becomes a $0.25 savings contribution. It’s a simple, powerful way to start building an emergency fund.

A Safe and Secure Way to Build Credit

When you arrive in Canada, you start with no credit history. A good credit score is essential for your future here—it affects your ability to get a phone plan, rent an apartment, and eventually get a mortgage.

Building credit usually requires getting a credit card, which can be difficult to obtain without a credit history. It also comes with the risk of accumulating high-interest debt if you’re not careful.

KOHO offers a safer alternative with its optional Credit Building feature. For a small monthly fee, you get a line of credit that KOHO helps you manage. Your consistent, on-time payments are reported to the major credit bureaus, allowing you to build a positive credit history in a controlled and predictable way. It’s the perfect way to establish your financial reputation in Canada without the risks of a traditional credit card.
