KOHO vs. High-Interest Savings Accounts: Where Should You Save in 2025?

Saving money is one of those goals that everyone has, but it can feel like a real challenge. You work hard for your money, and you want it to work hard for you in return. For a long time, the best place to make that happen was a High-Interest Savings Account (HISA). These accounts offered a way to grow your money faster than a standard savings account, keeping it safe and accessible.

But now, there are new tools designed not just to hold your money, but to help you actively save it. KOHO is at the forefront of this shift, blending spending and saving into one seamless experience. So, when it comes to growing your nest egg in 2025, where should your money go? Is a traditional HISA still the best option, or does KOHO offer a smarter way to save?

Let’s break down how KOHO’s savings features stack up against traditional High-Interest Savings Accounts. We'll explore interest rates, accessibility, and overall usability to help you decide which tool is the right fit for your savings goals.

What's the Difference? Active vs. Passive Saving

Before we get into a feature-by-feature comparison, it’s helpful to understand the core philosophy behind each option.

A High-Interest Savings Account (HISA) is a dedicated savings vehicle. Its primary job is to hold your money and pay you interest on your balance. Think of it as a safe parking spot for your cash where it can grow slowly and steadily over time. You deposit money, and the bank does the rest. It’s a very passive approach to saving.

A KOHO account is an all-in-one spending and saving tool. While it also offers a competitive interest rate on your balance, its main goal is to help you actively save. It’s designed with features that make saving a natural part of your everyday spending habits. It’s not just a place to park your money; it’s a system to help you find more money to park.

This distinction between passive holding and active saving is what makes this comparison so interesting.

The Savings Showdown: KOHO vs. HISA

Let's see how these two powerful savings tools compare in the areas that matter most.

1. Earning Interest: How Your Money Grows

The main reason to choose a savings tool is to earn interest. Let's look at how your money grows with each option.

  • High-Interest Savings Accounts: As the name suggests, this is their biggest strength. HISAs, especially those from online banks, are designed to offer some of the most competitive interest rates on the market. The rate is their main selling point. Your entire balance earns this rate, and it’s a great way to see your money grow without any effort on your part.
  • KOHO: KOHO also offers a great interest rate on the entire balance in your account, whether it's in your 'Spendable' or a dedicated Savings Goal. This means your money is always working for you, even the cash you’ve set aside for this week's groceries. It combines the earning power of a HISA with the flexibility of a spending account.

The Verdict: This one is often a close call. While some dedicated HISAs might offer a slightly higher promotional rate, KOHO provides a competitive rate on your entire balance—both spending and saving—making every dollar productive. It turns your whole account into a high-interest powerhouse.

2. The Saving Process: How You Actually Save Money

Growing your money isn’t just about interest rates; it’s about consistently putting money aside. How do these tools help you do that?

  • High-Interest Savings Accounts: A HISA is a destination for your money, but it doesn’t help you get it there. You have to manually transfer funds from your chequing account or set up a recurring automatic transfer. It relies entirely on your discipline to remember to save. If you forget, or if life gets in the way, your savings can stall.
  • KOHO: This is where KOHO changes the game. It’s packed with automated tools designed to make saving effortless. The RoundUps feature is a perfect example. It automatically rounds up your purchases to the nearest dollar (or more, if you choose) and tucks that spare change into your savings. A $4.50 coffee becomes a 50-cent savings contribution. It adds up incredibly fast without you even noticing. You can also set specific Savings Goals in the app and have KOHO automatically contribute to them.

The Verdict: KOHO is the clear winner here. It moves beyond passive saving and gives you active, automated tools that help you save money consistently. A HISA holds your savings, but KOHO helps you create them.

3. Accessibility and Usability

How easy is it to access and use your money when you need it?

  • High-Interest Savings Accounts: HISAs are designed for saving, not spending. They usually don't come with a debit card, and you can't pay bills directly from them. To use your money, you have to transfer it back to a chequing account, which can sometimes take a day or two. Some HISAs also have limits on the number of free withdrawals per month.
  • KOHO: KOHO combines your saving and spending into one account. The money in your 'Spendable' balance is available instantly via your KOHO Mastercard. If you need to access funds from a Savings Goal, you can transfer it to your Spendable balance in seconds, right from the app. This makes your savings highly accessible for emergencies without the slow transfer times of a traditional HISA.

The Verdict: KOHO offers far better accessibility. It removes the friction between saving and spending, giving you the flexibility to use your money when you need it without delays or penalties.

4. Fees and Hidden Costs

Fees can quickly cancel out the interest you earn. Let’s see how these accounts stack up.

  • High-Interest Savings Accounts: Most modern HISAs from online banks have no monthly fees, which is great. However, some may charge fees if you exceed your withdrawal limit or require other services. It’s important to read the fine print.
  • KOHO: The standard KOHO account is completely free. There are no monthly fees and no e-Transfer fees. Because it’s a prepaid system, you also avoid any risk of costly overdraft or NSF fees. KOHO does have optional premium tiers that come with a monthly fee, but these offer added benefits like higher cashback rates and no foreign transaction fees.

The Verdict: It's a tie. Both KOHO and the best HISAs offer excellent no-fee options. The key is to ensure you’re choosing a modern HISA that has eliminated unnecessary charges.

Which Savings Tool is Right for You?

Both KOHO and a High-Interest Savings Account are fantastic tools for growing your money. The best choice depends on your personal saving style and what you need from your account.

You should choose a High-Interest Savings Account if:

  • You are a disciplined saver. You already have a good system for regularly transferring money into savings and just need a place to park it.
  • You want to completely separate your savings. You prefer having your savings in a dedicated account that you’re not tempted to touch.
  • Your only goal is to find the absolute highest interest rate on the market for a large lump sum of money.

You should choose KOHO if:

  • You struggle to save consistently. You would benefit from automated tools like RoundUps that make saving effortless.
  • You want your everyday money to work for you. You like the idea of earning interest on your entire balance, including your spending money.
  • You value accessibility. You want to be able to access your savings quickly in an emergency without waiting for bank transfers.
  • You want an all-in-one tool. You love the idea of managing your spending, budgeting, and saving all from a single app.

The Final Word: Build Your Savings on Autopilot

A High-Interest Savings Account is a great, passive way to earn interest on money you’ve already managed to save. It’s a reliable and safe choice.

However, KOHO represents a more modern, active approach. It doesn’t just store your savings—it helps you build them. By integrating automated savings tools directly into your daily spending, KOHO helps you find money you didn’t even know you had. It turns every purchase into a small step toward your financial goals.

For many Canadians in 2025, the best strategy might be a combination of both. But if you’re looking for a single tool that empowers you to take control, makes saving feel easy, and rewards you along the way, KOHO is built for you. It’s time to let your account do the heavy lifting.
