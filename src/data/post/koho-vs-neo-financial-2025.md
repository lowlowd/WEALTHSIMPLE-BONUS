## Core Missions & Approach

- **KOHO** is designed to be your ultimate spending and saving partner. Think of it as a super-powered chequing account that helps you budget, save automatically, and earn cashback on your daily purchases. It's built around giving you complete control over your money in real-time, helping build better habits without risk of debt.
- **Neo Financial** is built around a rewards-first ecosystem. Their main goal: give you premium cashback rates like top credit cards, with the simplicity of a single card and app. Neo has built an extensive network of retail partners for impressive cashback rates on all kinds of purchases.

This fundamental difference is key: KOHO is a budgeting and saving tool that rewards you, while Neo is a rewards platform with spending and saving tools.

## Day-to-Day Account Features

Both platforms offer accounts for daily finances but function differently.

**The Verdict:** If your priority is strong budgeting habits and spending controls, KOHO shines. Neo caters more to maximized cashback and partner rewards.

### Rewards & Cashback

Getting cashback for spending is a huge perk. Both have strong rewards, but take different approaches:

- **KOHO:** Cashback on essentials and boosted rates via partner offers.
- **Neo:** Highest cashback rates at partner merchants; can rival/best premium cards.

### Budgeting & App Features

A modern fintech app should do more than show transactions; it should help you build healthy money habits.

**The Verdict:** KOHO is the clear winner if you want active budgeting, saving, and habit-building tools.

### Fees & Transparency

Surprise fees aren't cool! Both brands promise clear pricing:

- **KOHO:** No NSF/overdraft fees, no monthly fees on essentials plan.
- **Neo:** Fee structure varies by product; make sure to read fine print if upgrading.

**The Verdict:** KOHO has one of the most "transparent" models for everyday banking.

### Credit Building & Partner Offers

Neo excels at partner offers and credit products (credit card, high-interest savings).
