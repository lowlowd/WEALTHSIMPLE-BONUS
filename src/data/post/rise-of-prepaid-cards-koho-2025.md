## Why Are Prepaid Cards on the Rise?

The growing popularity of prepaid cards isn't a mystery. People are tired of the old way of doing things. They are actively seeking alternatives that offer more control, fewer fees, and a system that feels like it's on their side. The prepaid model directly addresses some of the biggest pain points of traditional banking.

### The Power of Spending Your Own Money

At its core, a prepaid card is simple: you load your own money onto it and can only spend what you have. This isn't a line of credit—it's your money, in your control. This simple concept has a profound psychological impact. It forces you to be mindful of your spending because you have a clear limit. This built-in boundary is the ultimate tool for anyone who has ever struggled with overspending or felt the sting of credit card debt. It makes impulse buys less tempting and budgeting a natural part of the process.

### A Welcome Escape from Hidden Fees

Traditional bank accounts can feel like a minefield of hidden charges. Monthly maintenance fees, e-Transfer limits, and—worst of all—non-sufficient funds (NSF) fees can chip away at your savings. An NSF fee, which can cost over $45, is a penalty for being short on cash, which feels fundamentally unfair.

Prepaid cards, especially modern ones like KOHO, are built on a foundation of transparency. Because you can't spend more than you have, you are protected from ever incurring an NSF fee. This safety net alone can save you hundreds of dollars and a lot of stress, making it an attractive alternative for anyone on a tight budget.
